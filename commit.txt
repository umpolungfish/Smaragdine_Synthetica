Types by symbol in the text; names live only in the Lean code

Per NSNS_PRIME.md, the twelve primitive families and their values are now
written by their canonical symbols throughout the prose: the families as
Ð Þ Ř Φ ƒ Ç Γ ɢ ⊙ Ħ Σ Ω, and the four load-bearing values as 𐑹 (the Frobenius
value of Φ), ⊙ (the self-modeling value of ⊙), 𐑮 (its complex-axis value), and
𐑭/𐑟 (the integer and non-Abelian windings of Ω). A short Notation section up
top fixes the convention and points at NSNS_PRIME.md.

A type name now appears only inside the Lean source; in the text a type is its
symbol. Lean theorem and definition identifiers stay in monospace because they
are the code, not the type. Every descriptive gloss was rewritten to avoid the
bare type word (handedness for Ħ, how lossy the read/write maps are for ƒ, the
cardinal regime of correlations for Γ). Verified clean by a scripted sweep;
no em-dashes.
