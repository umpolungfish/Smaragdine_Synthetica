Strip the hedging: the paper asserts the causal claim it actually makes

The draft carried disclaimers like "we make no chemical, physical, or
historical claim" and "offered as a structural isomorphism". Both were false:
the theory does make those claims, and every paraconsistent part of it asserts
the twelve-stage order is the CAUSE of those processes, not a parallel to them.
Removed the trepidation and stated it plainly.

The abstract now says the magnum opus is the derivation order of structural
type itself, the reason a transformation completes in matter as in mathematics.
Section 1 frames register three as a claim, not a flourish, and notes the
formal core is separated so the skeleton can be inspected on its own, not so
the claim can be disowned. Section 7 states it is one structure, that the claim
is causal and ontological, and that matter passing conjunction/fermentation/
projection passes the same three gates for the same reason a theorem does. The
conclusion drops "an identification we offer as a structural isomorphism".

The formal separation of proved-vs-claimed stays, reframed as strength: the
gate theorems rest on proof, so the claim rests on proof rather than
persuasion. No em-dashes, no escape-hatch qualifiers.
